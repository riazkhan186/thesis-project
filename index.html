1.2 Motivation

Bangladesh is a developing country. Family planning remains one of the top priorities in Bangladesh’s Fourth Health Sector Program 20, 2017-2012, towards achieving sustainable development goals (2019). Despite past successes, the family planning program in Bangladesh faces several challenges. Also, FP and RH programs face implementation and institutional challenges. Suboptimal availability of contraceptives is a major obstacle, leading to higher contraceptive breaks resulting in unplanned pregnancy. Also, contraceptives are funded mostly by donors, and the government needs to have a plan in place to protect their finances in the long run. The division of FP and RH services across the seven operational plans weakens the coordination between the two programs, which is exacerbated by the acute shortage of human resources for health. So it is very important for all married couples, especially newly married couples, to become move aware of reproductive health and family planning. Promoting family planning and ensuring access to the desired contraceptive system for women and couples is essential to supporting women’s health and development while protecting women’s health and autonomy. By reducing the rate of involuntary pregnancy, family planning also reduces the need for unsafe a abortion.
